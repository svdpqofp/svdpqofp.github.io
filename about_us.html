St. Vincent de Paul Society - Queen of Peace Conference

Our Mission Statement:


Q of P Vincentians: serving the community of Mesa, AZ since 1954.


The Society of St. Vincent de Paul is dedicated to feeding, clothing, housing and healing individuals and families in our community who have nowhere else to turn for help. SVdP provides meaningful opportunities for volunteers to serve their neighbours in need with love and compassion. We provide help for people's physical, mental, and spiritual needs.

Members of the Society of St. Vincent de Paul (Vincentians) are men and women striving to grow spiritually by offering personal service to those in their local community in need.

Queen of Peace Vincentians represent all ages, races, and incomes, and have a unique awareness that it is their duty to share their many blessings with their neighbours in need.

History:


The Beginnings of The Society of St. Vincent de Paul


"The Society of St. Vincent de Paul originated in France during the midst of early 19th century turmoil. As a young student at the Sorbonne, Antoine Frédéric Ozanam was appalled by the conditions he saw in Paris. At a student meeting, another student challenged Ozanam and other practicing Catholics, admitting that while the Catholic Church had done much good work in the past, he could not see what good the Church was doing currently. On April 23, 1833, his 20th birthday, Ozanam invited five other students to a meeting, where they founded the first Conference of Charity to assist the poor. This Catholic lay organization was intended to help members increase in holiness by loving service to those in need. Shortly thereafter, they changed their name to the Society of St. Vincent de Paul in honor of their patron saint." (US SVDP Site)

Programs include:



  • Services for the homeless.

  • Medical and dental care for the working poor.

  • Charity dining rooms that serve thousands each day.

  • Food boxes for hungry families.

  • Thrift stores throughout the region.

  • A bridge housing shelter.

  • General assistance for individuals in need.

"No act of charity is foreign to the society."
